How Features Relate to Diagnosis

🔴 High-Risk Indicators

  • Large radius & area (worst)
  • High concavity values
  • Elevated compactness
  • Many concave points

🟢 Benign Indicators

  • Smaller radius mean
  • Low concavity & compactness
  • Smooth perimeter
  • Symmetric nucleus shape

ℹ️ Measurement Types

  • Mean: Average across cells
  • SE: Std. error of the mean
  • Worst: Largest 3 values mean
💚 Prevention & Early Detection

Protecting Your Health

Early detection significantly improves survival rates. Know your screening options and stay proactive.

🩺

Regular Mammograms

Women 40+ should have annual mammograms. Early imaging catches tumors before symptoms appear.

🤲

Self-Examination

Perform monthly breast self-exams to identify new lumps, skin changes, or nipple discharge.

🥗

Healthy Diet

Eat fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Limit processed foods and alcohol consumption.

🏃

Physical Activity

At least 150 min of moderate exercise per week can reduce breast cancer risk by up to 10–20%.

🚭

Avoid Tobacco

Smoking is linked to increased risk. Quitting at any age reduces cancer risk substantially.

🧬

Genetic Counseling

BRCA1/BRCA2 carriers have higher risk. Genetic testing helps personalize your prevention plan.

🌙

Quality Sleep

Adequate sleep supports immune function and hormone regulation, both linked to cancer risk.

👩‍⚕️

Clinical Exams

Discuss your risk with a doctor every 1–3 years in your 20s–30s, annually from age 40.

⚠️ Risk Factors

Know Your Risk

Understanding modifiable and non-modifiable risk factors empowers you to take action where possible.

Modifiable Risk Factors You can change these
  • 🍷
    Alcohol Consumption
    Even 1 drink/day increases risk by 7–10%
  • ⚖️
    Obesity (post-menopause)
    Excess body fat raises estrogen levels
  • 🛋️
    Physical Inactivity
    Sedentary lifestyle is a recognized risk factor
  • 💊
    Hormone Therapy (HRT)
    Prolonged use of combined HRT increases risk
  • 🚬
    Smoking
    Particularly harmful for younger women
  • 🍔
    Poor Diet
    High-fat diets linked to elevated risk
Non-Modifiable Risk Factors Awareness is key
  • 🧬
    BRCA1 / BRCA2 Mutations
    Up to 72% lifetime risk for BRCA1 carriers
  • 👩‍👩‍👧
    Family History
    First-degree relative doubles the risk
  • 🎂
    Age
    Risk increases significantly after age 50
  • 🩸
    Dense Breast Tissue
    Makes tumors harder to detect on mammograms
  • 🌸
    Early Menstruation / Late Menopause
    Longer estrogen exposure increases risk
  • 🏥
    Previous Benign Conditions
    Atypical hyperplasia raises future risk

❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Fine Needle Aspiration (FNA) is a minimally invasive diagnostic procedure where cells are extracted from a breast lump. The Wisconsin dataset digitizes the cell nuclei images and computes 30 features per sample. The dataset was created by Dr. William H. Wolberg at the University of Wisconsin.
IBM AutoAI achieves ~96–98% accuracy on the Wisconsin dataset depending on the pipeline selected. However, this is a research/educational tool. No AI model is infallible. Clinical diagnosis requires physician evaluation, imaging, and often biopsy.
For each FNA image, multiple cell nuclei are measured. Mean is the average of all measured nuclei. SE (Standard Error) measures spread/variability. Worst is the mean of the three largest values — capturing extreme characteristics that may indicate malignancy.
